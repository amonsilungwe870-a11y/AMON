Formulas to Consider Under Work

W = f × d
W = work done
f = force applied
d = displacement

  • w = mgh: Work due to gravity
  • w = 1/2 mv²: Work due to kinetic energy
  • w = 1/2 ke²: Work due to elastic potential energy
  • w = fd cos(θ): Work when force is applied at an angle
  • w = ΔKE: Work due to change in kinetic energy
  • w = ΔPE: Work due to change in potential energy
  • Work can also be calculated by w = ΔE, the change in energy of the system
  • If force does not cause displacement, then work done is zero
  • Work can be positive, negative, or zero depending on the direction of force and displacement
  • If force is constant and the object moves in a straight line, use w = f d cos(θ)
  • Work is measured in joules (J) in the SI system
